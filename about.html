Our History

Founded in 1995, the Institute for Water Conservation emerged in response to growing concerns over water scarcity and environmental degradation in the face of rapid urbanization and climate change. Initially established as a small coalition of scientists, policymakers, and community advocates, the institute began with a series of local workshops aimed at educating communities about sustainable water use.

In 2000, the institute expanded its reach by partnering with regional governments and non-profits to launch groundwater recharge initiatives, addressing the critical issue of declining aquifer levels. By 2010, we had developed a robust research program, pioneering technologies for water purification and efficient irrigation systems, which were adopted by farmers and municipalities across multiple states.

In 2015, the institute received national recognition for its innovative approaches to water management, earning the prestigious Environmental Stewardship Award. This accolade fueled our growth, enabling us to establish international collaborations to tackle global water challenges. Over the years, we’ve worked with over 200 organizations worldwide, impacting millions through education, policy advocacy, and on-the-ground projects.

Today, the Institute for Water Conservation continues to lead in research, education, and advocacy, driving solutions that balance human needs with environmental sustainability. Our work includes promoting water-smart technologies, supporting equitable water access, and fostering community-driven conservation efforts.
