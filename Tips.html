Tips

Indoors

Fix leaks promptly: A dripping faucet can waste gallons daily. Check faucets, pipes, and toilets regularly. Use low-flow fixtures: Install aerators on faucets, low-flow showerheads, and dual-flush toilets to reduce water use. Shorten showers: Aim for 5-minute showers. Turn off water while soaping or shampooing. Turn off taps: Shut off water while brushing teeth, shaving, or washing dishes by hand. Run full loads: Only use washing machines and dishwashers with full loads to maximize efficiency. Collect water: Reuse water from rinsing vegetables or dehumidifiers for plants.

Outdoors

Water plants wisely: Water early morning or late evening to reduce evaporation. Use drip irrigation or soaker hoses. Mulch gardens: Add mulch to retain soil moisture and reduce watering needs. Choose native plants: Plant drought-resistant or native species that require less water. Sweep, don’t hose: Use a broom instead of a hose to clean driveways or sidewalks. Check sprinklers: Adjust sprinklers to avoid watering pavement. Install rain sensors to prevent unnecessary watering.

Harvest rainwater: Use rain barrels to collect runoff for gardening. Upgrade appliances: Choose water-efficient models (look for WaterSense labels). Monitor usage: Track your water bill to spot unusual spikes and adjust habits. Educate others: Share conservation tips with family or roommates to maximize impact.
